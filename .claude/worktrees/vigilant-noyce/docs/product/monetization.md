# Monetization Strategy

## Overview

Gymory is a high-intent discovery platform.

Users come to Gymory with a clear goal:
- find a gym
- check equipment
- decide where to train

This makes Gymory fundamentally different from content or entertainment apps.

The primary value is not traffic — it is **decision intent**.

---

## Core Monetization Principle

> Monetize decisions, not impressions.

Avoid relying on:
- banner ads
- generic ad networks
- low-value CPM traffic

Focus on:
- gym discovery
- lead generation
- visibility
- conversion

---

## Monetization Phases

---

## Phase 1 — No Monetization (MVP)

### Goal
- build data
- build usage
- validate search behavior

### Strategy
- no ads
- no paid features
- focus entirely on:
  - data coverage
  - search quality
  - user experience

### Why
Without data and usage, monetization has no value.

---

## Phase 2 — Featured Listings

### Description

Gyms can pay to:
- appear higher in search results
- be highlighted in listings
- be shown as "Recommended"

### Example UI

- "Featured"
- "Popular nearby"
- "Best equipped gyms"

### Revenue Model

- monthly subscription (e.g. $30–$200/month depending on region)
- or pay-per-location

### Why It Works

Users are already looking for a gym.

This is high-intent traffic:
- conversion probability is high
- gyms benefit directly

---

## Phase 3 — Lead Generation

### Description

Capture and pass leads to gyms:

- free trial sign-up
- day pass inquiry
- contact requests

### Example

- "Get a free trial"
- "Try this gym today"
- "Contact gym"

### Revenue Model

- cost per lead (CPL)
- cost per acquisition (CPA)

### Why It Works

- users are ready to take action
- gyms pay for real prospects

---

## Phase 4 — Gym Owner Tools (SaaS-lite)

### Description

Provide tools for gym owners:

- manage gym profile
- update equipment inventory
- show peak hours
- track views / clicks
- manage promotions

### Revenue Model

- subscription
- tiered plans

### Example Plans

- Free: basic listing
- Pro: featured + analytics
- Premium: full profile + promotion tools

---

## Phase 5 — Data & Insights (Advanced)

### Description

Use aggregated data to provide insights:

- equipment trends
- demand by area
- popular gym features
- competitive benchmarking

### Revenue Model

- B2B analytics
- reports for gym chains or investors

---

## Ads Strategy (Optional)

### Recommendation

Avoid ads in early stages.

### If used:

Only use:
- native ads (inside lists)
- non-intrusive placements

Avoid:
- popups
- interstitials
- disruptive UX

### Why

Gymory is a utility product.
Users want fast decisions, not distractions.

---

## Pricing Strategy (Initial Suggestion)

### Featured Listing

- small gym: $30–$50/month
- mid-tier gym: $50–$100/month
- premium gym: $100–$200/month

### Lead Generation

- $2–$10 per lead (region dependent)

---

## Key Metrics to Track

### Product Metrics
- searches per user
- filter usage
- gym detail views
- submission rate

### Business Metrics
- click-through rate (CTR) on gyms
- lead conversion rate
- featured listing conversion
- retention of gyms

---

## Monetization Readiness Checklist

Before enabling monetization:

- [ ] at least 50–100 gyms in database
- [ ] consistent search usage
- [ ] users clicking into gym detail pages
- [ ] reliable equipment data
- [ ] basic trust indicators (verified / updated)

---

## Long-Term Vision

Gymory evolves into:

- a gym discovery platform
- a training-focused search engine
- a data layer for gym infrastructure

Monetization aligns with:
- helping users find the right gym
- helping gyms reach the right users

---

## Summary

Gymory should monetize:

- visibility
- intent
- decisions

Not:

- traffic volume
- ad impressions
