# Gymory — Marketing Strategy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last updated:** 2026-05-14  
**Status:** Living document

---

## 1. Positioning

Gymory should be positioned as the Hong Kong training infrastructure database, not just a generic gym directory.

**Core promise:** Find gyms with the equipment you need — not just the nearest one.

**Sharper market position:**
- Google Maps for gym equipment
- OpenRice-style discovery for serious gym-goers
- A community-maintained database for Hong Kong training culture

**Primary niches:**
- HYROX
- Powerlifting
- Bodybuilding
- Hybrid training
- Serious strength training

This is where Gymory can differentiate. General gym discovery is crowded, but equipment-level truth for serious training is underserved in Hong Kong.

---

## 2. Growth Thesis

Gymory's growth should come from a loop:

1. SEO and short-form content bring in high-intent gym-goers.
2. Users land on equipment, district, brand, or gym pages.
3. Missing data is shown honestly instead of hidden.
4. Users are prompted to contribute equipment information.
5. Better equipment data makes the pages more useful.
6. More useful pages rank better and create better social content.

The product should monetize decisions, not impressions. Do not prioritize display ads early. The valuable moment is when a user is choosing where to train.

---

## 3. SEO Strategy

SEO is the highest-leverage growth channel because Gymory has structured data that maps naturally to long-tail search intent.

### 3.1 Search Intent

Target searches include:
- "Hong Kong hack squat gym"
- "HYROX gym HK"
- "gym with Eleiko Hong Kong"
- "Sham Shui Po powerlifting gym"
- "Mong Kok gym deadlift"
- "gym with sled push Hong Kong"
- "香港 gym 有 ski erg"
- "香港邊間 gym 有 hack squat"
- "香港 HYROX gym"

These users are not casually browsing. They already know what they need and are trying to choose a gym.

### 3.2 Equipment Landing Pages

Create indexable landing pages for high-intent equipment.

**Examples:**
- `/en/equipment/hack-squat`
- `/en/equipment/ski-erg`
- `/en/equipment/power-rack`
- `/zh-HK/equipment/hack-squat`

**Page content:**
- Gyms that have the equipment
- District filter or district grouping
- Nearby gyms where location is available
- Verified gyms surfaced first
- Equipment counts where available
- Missing-data CTA where equipment is unknown

**First-batch topics:**
- Hack squat
- Ski erg
- Sled
- Power rack
- Deadlift platform
- Assault bike
- Rower
- Wall ball
- Heavy dumbbells
- Eleiko

### 3.3 District + Equipment Pages

District and equipment combinations are the programmatic SEO opportunity, but they should be rolled out carefully to avoid thin pages.

**Examples:**
- "Best gyms with Hack Squat in Mong Kok"
- "HYROX gyms in Hong Kong"
- "Powerlifting gyms in Kowloon"
- "Gyms with SkiErg in Central"

**Rule:** Only index pages that have enough useful data or editorial context. Empty or near-empty pages should not be added to the sitemap.

### 3.4 Training Collection Pages

Gymory should own training-use-case pages, especially where equipment requirements are specific.

**Priority pages:**
- Best HYROX gyms in Hong Kong
- Best powerlifting gyms in Hong Kong
- Best gyms for bodybuilding in Hong Kong
- Best hybrid training gyms in Hong Kong
- Gyms with sled push in Hong Kong
- Gyms with SkiErg in Hong Kong

These pages should be data-backed: racks, platforms, sleds, ski ergs, rowers, wall balls, heavy dumbbells, equipment brands, and verification status.

### 3.5 Comparison Articles

Comparison content should be used once there is enough data to support it.

**Examples:**
- Pure vs Anytime Fitness for strength training
- Best gyms in Hong Kong for HYROX training
- Top gyms with Eleiko equipment in Hong Kong
- Best 24-hour gyms for serious training in Hong Kong

The tone should be practical and evidence-based. Avoid unsupported claims. Use Gymory data as the reason the article exists.

## 4. Short-Form Content Strategy

IG, TikTok, and Reels can create faster traction than SEO and should feed the SEO loop.

### 4.1 Core Format: "Which Hong Kong gym has X?"

This should be the main recurring format.

**Examples:**
- "香港有邊間 gym 有 Hack Squat?"
- "香港邊間 gym 啞鈴最重?"
- "香港邊間 gym 最適合 HYROX?"
- "香港邊間 gym 可以 deadlift?"
- "香港 gym 有 SkiErg 嗎?"

Each video should point to the matching Gymory landing page or gym list.

### 4.2 Gym Walkthrough Reels

Walkthrough content is useful because it shows real equipment, not marketing copy.

**Shot list:**
- Entrance
- Racks
- Platforms
- Dumbbells
- Machines
- Cardio area
- Sled
- Ski erg
- Rowers
- Wall balls

**Overlay data:**
- District
- Monthly fee or day pass if known
- Best for: HYROX, powerlifting, bodybuilding, general fitness
- Key equipment
- "Full equipment list on Gymory"

### 4.3 Expectation vs Reality

This content can be more shareable and meme-friendly.

**Examples:**
- "When a gym says they have a powerlifting setup..."
- "When the gym says deadlift-friendly but there is one rack"
- "When the plates are all mixed and nobody knows where the 1.25s went"
- "When a gym has a sled but nowhere to push it"

The goal is not to attack gyms. The tone should be funny, accurate, and relatable.

### 4.4 HYROX and Powerlifting Niche Content

HYROX, hybrid training, and powerlifting are the strongest early communities because they care about specific equipment.

**Content angles:**
- "What makes a gym HYROX-friendly?"
- "Minimum equipment for powerlifting training"
- "Hong Kong gyms with sled, ski erg, rower, and wall balls"
- "Best districts for serious strength training"

## 5. Community Growth

The main MVP growth constraint is not technology. It is filling and verifying equipment data.

### 5.1 Missing Data as CTA

Gymory should show missing data honestly and convert it into contribution prompts.

**Examples:**
- "Equipment details missing"
- "Help verify this gym"
- "No data yet — be the first to add it"
- "Trained here recently? Add what you saw"

This is better than pretending the database is complete.

### 5.2 Contributor Status

Fitness communities often respond well to status, recognition, and being useful to other serious trainers.

**Planned mechanics:**
- First contributor badge
- Verified contributor status
- Top contributors this month
- Most verified submissions
- Most accurate spotter

These should come after the basic contribution flow is smooth.

### 5.3 Community Message

The product copy should reinforce that contributions help the Hong Kong training community.

**Suggested framing:**
- "Help the HK training community find gyms with the right equipment."
- "Seen newer equipment info? Send an update for review."
- "Equipment data is contributed by gym-goers."

## 6. Partnerships

Partnerships should come after there is enough traffic and user intent to make Gymory valuable to gyms.

**Potential partners:**
- Independent gyms
- HYROX gyms
- Powerlifting gyms
- Bodybuilding gyms
- PT studios

**Potential offers:**
- Featured listing
- Verified badge
- Gym profile optimization
- Lead generation
- PT lead placement
- Promoted placement on relevant equipment or district pages

Do not prioritize display ads early. The better monetization path is helping gyms and trainers appear at the moment users are choosing where to train.

## 8. Success Metrics

**SEO:**
- Indexed landing pages
- Organic clicks by equipment/district query
- Ranking movement for high-intent terms
- Click-through rate from search results

**Content:**
- Reel views
- Saves
- Shares
- Profile clicks
- Landing page visits from social

**Community:**
- Equipment submissions per week
- Accepted submissions
- Percentage of active gyms with at least one equipment signal
- Number of gyms with recently verified equipment data
- Repeat contributors

**Business:**
- Gym owner inbound requests
- Featured listing interest
- PT / gym lead inquiries
- Conversion from landing pages to gym contact clicks
